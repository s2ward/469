Some 'special sequences', e.g. 1a and 1b is the same book, but one sequence within has another starting point - so the books can be rearranged to match eachother 1:1.

At this point, all books are original. Some have been split/elongated which is marked with '+' signs, but no book is rearranged.

After 10's of hours, i managed to join the bigger sequences to create one huge sequence, but the last remaining books (on the top right) are quite 'scrambled' and have not been put in place yet.

I numbered each book [B1...72] as they appeared here in somewhat of an order, this is because i believe some books might have to be unscrambled and rearranged later on. with numberings, one could do this more systematically.
Though I believe that one needs to find the huge line first, and rearrange later - as one small mistake or wrong rearrangement could change the outcome of the final sequence, and we dont want that at all.

It anyhow feels like Knightmare had one huge sequence and made some kind of 'art' with it by copy pasting and elongating sequences.

I believe he scrambled some sequences to make it difficult to put the pieces together into one huge line. It also serves as a safeguard from bruteforcing.
In a handful of books, a single number has been cut out - this seems intentional and also serves as entropy to guard against bruteforce attempts.

One other thing that the scramblement does, is it shows us what sequences are its own 'word/number', e.g. 451.

Some books feel 'special' with interesting properties, like the book containing '666' and the book with '469', these seem heavily scrambled - and neither 666 nor 469 seem to be bonelord numbers.
